What are kidneys?

The kidneys are organs responsible for:
  • maintaining normal concentrations of electrolytes in the bloodstream
  • filtering blood and ridding toxic and excess electrolytes in the form of urine

    • Most people are born with two kidneys...

    But we know that one can live a long life with only one good kidney.

    • As you can see, they are located near the back, and mostly covered by your ribcage, muscle, and fat.
    • Traumatic injury to the kidney commonly occurs after a blow to the back (i.e. kick or punch).

    How do kidneys work?

    Each kidney consists of millions of nephrons which work side-by-side.

    Each nephron acts as part of an assembly line, each part with its own purpose.

    The initial part is called the glomerulus, and it filters the blood, like a collander filters pasta in boiling water.

    The fluid that passes through the collander is like the urine that the kidney ultimately sends to the bladder.

    The pasta that stays behind is like the cells and protein that the healthy kidney recirculates back into the bloodstream.

    How do we know that kidneys work?

    Most people with kidney disease feel fine, and have no symptoms.

    Unfortunately, our physical exam is an insensitive way to know if someone has kidney disease.

    Fortunately, we have tests and imaging studies that can answer this question with 100% certainty.
